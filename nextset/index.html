nextset — Privacy Policy

Effective July 11, 2026

What nextset is

nextset is an iOS strength-training app that runs your program — 5/3/1 and similar progressions — entirely on your phone.

Data we collect

nextset has no accounts, ads, product analytics, or nextset backend. Your training data never leaves your phone unless you export it yourself or turn on Apple Health saving (below). Some release builds may offer optional crash diagnostics as described below; those diagnostics never include your programs, weights, sets, or workout history.

Where your data lives

Everything you enter — programs, logged sets, custom templates, settings — is stored in a small local database and key-value storage inside the app's private container on your device. Like any app data, it is included in your own iPhone backups (iCloud or a computer), which you control. I have no copy and no way to get one.

Apple Health

Off by default. If you turn on Settings → Privacy → "Save workouts to Apple Health", iOS asks for write permission, and from then on each finished workout is written to Apple Health as a strength-training workout: the start and end time, plus the program name, day name, total volume, and set count as metadata. That is the whole list — no invented calorie estimates. nextset never reads anything from Health and never asks for read access. To stop, flip the switch off, or revoke access in the Health app (your profile → Apps → nextset). Workouts already saved are part of your Health data; delete them in Health if you want them gone.

Crash reports

Crash reporting is optional and uses Sentry. When it is available in a release build and enabled in Settings, an app crash may send a stack trace, app version, iOS version, device model, and technical identifiers needed to group the same crash. The reports are not linked to your identity and are not used for advertising or tracking. nextset disables Sentry's default personal-data collection, session tracking, performance tracing, screenshots, and view-hierarchy capture. It never attaches your training data. You can turn Crash reports off in Settings; turning it off takes full effect the next time the app launches.

If crash reporting is not configured in a build, or if it is disabled, nextset sends no diagnostics. Development builds and Expo Go do not send crash reports.

Deleting your data

Settings → "Erase all data" wipes everything the app has stored: settings, programs, custom templates, any in-progress session, and the full workout history, including the local database. Deleting the app does the same — all data lives in the app's container, and iOS removes it with the app. If you used Settings → "Export data" first, that JSON file is yours: it lives wherever you sent it, and I never receive a copy.

Children

nextset is not directed at children under 13.
