Fitly — Privacy Policy

Effective July 10, 2026

What Fitly is

Fitly is a nutrition-tracking app: you log what you eat, keep recipes, set calorie and macro goals, and can optionally chat with an AI assistant about your log. It is built for iPhone (the same app can also run on Android and the web). Unlike a purely on-device app, Fitly has accounts: the app talks to a server I run, and your data is stored there so it stays with your account.

Data we collect

Account. When you register, Fitly asks for your first and last name, your email address, and a password. The name is stored as a display name you can edit or remove in Profile. Your password is never stored in readable form in Fitly's database — depending on how the server is configured, either the server keeps only a salted scrypt hash of it, or sign-in is delegated to Supabase (see Third-party services) and the password is passed through to Supabase and not stored by Fitly at all.

Food and nutrition data. This is the point of the app, and it is stored on the server under your account: your daily food log (which foods, how much, which meal — breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack — and when each entry was logged), your recipes (ingredients, steps, servings, tags, notes, and the source link if you imported one), recurring meals and their weekly schedule, any custom foods you create (including name, barcode, serving sizes, and nutrition facts), your calorie and macro goals and goal label, and display preferences (theme, units, how macros are shown).

AI assistant connection (optional). If you connect an AI provider in Settings, the server stores which provider you chose, your chosen model, and your API key or sign-in tokens for that provider, encrypted at rest (the last four characters are kept readable so the app can label the connection). The server also keeps a record of actions the assistant proposed — what it wanted to log and whether you confirmed, cancelled, or it failed. Your chat messages are not stored on the server; the conversation lives in the app while it is open and is sent along with each message you send.

Automatic. The server keeps diagnostic logs of API requests: timestamp, endpoint, your account id, your device's IP address as the server sees it, response status and timing, and a size-capped copy of the request body with passwords, tokens, and keys redacted. Error reports are described under Third-party services. That is all. Fitly does not ask for your location, contacts, photos, or Apple Health data, and contains no advertising or analytics identifiers.

Where it's stored

Your account and food data live in a PostgreSQL database on the server I operate for Fitly. On your phone, the app keeps: your sign-in session (email plus access tokens) in the device's secure keychain storage, a cached copy of your preferences, and any draft you type into the Help & feedback form — that form saves only to your device and sends nothing. AI chat history is held in memory only and is gone when the app restarts. Deleting the app removes everything stored on the phone, but not the copy on the server — see Deleting your account and data.

Third-party services

Supabase (sign-in). Fitly's server supports two sign-in setups: one where it verifies your email and password itself, and one where sign-in is handled by Supabase, a hosted authentication service. In the Supabase setup, your email and password go to Supabase to create and check your account, Supabase issues the tokens that keep you signed in, and it may send you an account-confirmation email. Supabase sees nothing else — none of your food data.

Sentry (error reporting). Both the app and the server use Sentry, for errors only — there is no performance tracing or session analytics, and the "send personal data" option is off on both sides. If the app hits an error, Sentry receives the error details, a breadcrumb trail of recent API calls (endpoint, status, and timing — not their contents), and, in installed builds, a screen replay of the moments leading up to that error; replays are captured only when an error occurs. When the server hits an error, the report is tagged with your account id (never your email) and a request id so I can find the matching log line.

Your AI provider (optional, only if you connect one). The assistant runs on an account you bring: an OpenAI API key, an Anthropic API key, or a ChatGPT subscription you sign into on OpenAI's site. When you chat, my server sends that provider your messages, the current date, and whatever the assistant looks up to answer you — which can include your daily food log with its macros and goals, and food-catalog search results. This happens under your own account with that provider, and their privacy terms govern what they do with it. The assistant cannot change your data without an in-app confirmation from you, and disconnecting the provider in Settings deletes your stored key or tokens from the server.

Recipe sites you import from. If you paste a recipe link, my server fetches that page to extract the recipe. The site sees a request from my server, not from your phone, and nothing about you is sent with it.

Food catalog sources. The shared food catalog is built from USDA FoodData Central and Open Food Facts data using scripts I run separately. Neither the app nor the server sends them anything about you.

There is nothing else: no analytics SDKs, no ad networks, no payment processors, and no push-notification services in either the app or the server.

How your data is used

To run the app: show your log, total your macros against your goals, keep recipes and recurring meals working, have your data there when you sign in on a new device, and power the assistant when you ask it something. Also to fix bugs, via the logs and error reports described above. That is the whole list. I do not sell your data, show ads, track you across other apps or sites, build analytics profiles, or use your data to train anything. What your chosen AI provider does with chats sent under your own account is governed by their terms, not mine.

Deleting your account and data

In the app you can delete individual things at any time: log entries, recipes, custom foods and serving sizes, recurring meals, and the AI provider connection (Settings → AI provider → Disconnect, which deletes your stored key or tokens from the server). Signing out clears the session from your device.

There is currently no in-app button that deletes your whole account. To delete your account and everything tied to it — profile, preferences, food logs, recipes, custom foods, and any stored AI credentials — email anthonymtamasi@gmail.com from the address on the account, and I will delete it from the server and confirm when it is done. Deleting the app from your phone removes the local data but does not delete the account on the server.

Data retention

Your data is kept for as long as your account exists, so it is there when you sign back in. Expired sign-in tokens are purged automatically. Diagnostic logs and error reports exist for debugging, not analytics; I keep them only as long as they are useful for that and do not build archives from them. When you delete something in the app, it is deleted from the database, not flagged and kept; when I delete your account on request, everything tied to it goes with it.

Children

Fitly is not directed at children under 13.
